Milan

Day 1

We stayed in Milan for one night. It is a very busy city, kind of like New York. Our purpose here was to see the painting of "the last supper" the next day. To pass the time, we went to the main square and saw the city's cathedral. The big thing at this one is high up on the alter is a red light where a nail is placed. They claim it to be one of the nails driven into Jesus's hands. You really can only see the red light though as it is so high up. There's also two tombs of cardinals. The creepy thing is that they are made of glass so you can see the bodies. I didn't have to look at the tombs for too long. For dinner we found a cafeteria. I didn't recognize the names of any of the dishes so I guessed and ordered and hoped for the best. Turns out I ordered shrimp and rice. Not beautiful since I don't eat fish-but Trent was good and switched me for his pasta dish. He then pulled out our dictionary and looked up all the words for fish so I wouldn't make that mistake again. The next morning was crazy, we packed our bags, left them in the luggage area at the hotel, and ran out to catch a metro. Of course it had to be raining, the hotelier said it was unusual as they never get rain. But rain couldn't stop us today. Once we got off the metro, we were completely lost. No matter how many streets we looked at, we could not orient ourselves on the map with where we needed to be. Time was running out. Our reservation for "the last supper" was set for 9:30am and we had to pick up the tickets 20 min before or else they would give our spot away to someone else. Finally we found the street we needed to be on, gave up on holding the umbrella, and swam through the rain. With a few more wrong turns we got to the church, waited our turn to get our tickets, and looked down at a sign saying we were at the wrong entrance. So out in the rain we flew to the actual entrance. We were quite the sight as we were drenched from head to toe but flinging our jackets off as we sweated profusely. It was 9:25 and I just knew we were too late. I walked up to the ticket window, said my name, and prepared to be told that our spot was gone. To my utter shock, he handed me two tickets and pointed us where to go. Good thing we were seeing the last supper, not the first supper or we would have totally missed it! Ok I learned some really cool things about this painting that I just have to share. In the room, you get 15 minutes then swiftly ushered back out. We had an English guide tell us about the painting by Leonardo da Vinci. It was incredible. She told us about the history and how it survived the bombs and raids of WWII. She told us about the technique da Vinci used. Though the paint he used began to fade in just 7 years, he chose this particular paint as it could be worked with over a longer period of time. Da Vinci needed to take his time, to really mold the picture the way he felt it needed to be portrayed. The guide specifically pointed out the apostle judas. Before this piece, judas was always painted on the other side of the table as if to say that he was foreordained to betray Jesus, but Leonardo put him on the same side, so as to say he still had free agency. We are not destined to any fault without going through our free choice. The guide also pointed our Jesus's stance. The interesting thing is on the opposite side of the room is a painting of the crucifixion. Jesus's hands are outstretched in both the Last Supper and the crucifixion. Even though his feet are now cut out of the picture, history tells us that his feet were painted one on top of the other, in the same position on the cross signifying his knowledge of what is to come. I could not stop staring at this art piece. Before it was just a familiar picture I had seen over and over, now it is more than that to me.
